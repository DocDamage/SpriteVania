# Story Outline

The Black Keep is a demonic castle rooted in the real world near modern city
outskirts and a rural swamp road. Its portals open into pocket dimensions from
different points in time and space.

The Keep is ruled by layered forces: a cursed royal line, a cult, a magical
military order, a living demonic Core, and a deeper ancient evil older than the
Keep itself.

## Goals

- Establish The Black Keep as the main identity.
- Keep the SpriteVania vertical slice as implementation foundation.
- Make Feudal Japan fragments the strongest early story flavor.
- Support a recruitable, player-named party.
- Build toward World Break and final Core choices.
- Avoid final dialogue until character voice is locked.

## Inspiration Rule

Use older Chroma's Edge and Orion material only as inspiration.

Keep as inspiration:

- World Break structure.
- False relic and true relic misdirection.
- Act 1 catastrophe and Act 2 restoration.
- Affinity or pair-combo idea.
- Large systems thinking for equipment, accessibility, settings, saves,
  endgame, and optional bosses.
- The idea that major powers stabilize or destabilize reality.

Replace completely:

- Orion as setting.
- The Lattice and Progenitor Engine names.
- Nix as the only activation or interface character.
- Dominion as named faction.
- The fixed 13 Chroma's Edge party members.
- ATB combat.
- Summon-owner canon.

## Core Premise

The Black Keep appears near the edge of modern civilization. Roads bend toward
swamp, swamp bends toward castle stone, and the Keep opens portals into time
fragments, ruined worlds, and living nightmares.

The heroes are seal-bearers. They can stabilize, misuse, or break the Keep's
portal systems. Their progress gives them power, but also makes them useful to
the forces manipulating the castle.

## Act Structure

Act 1: Intrusion.

- Modern world contact.
- Swamp transition.
- Castle Gate arrival.
- Witch recruitment.
- Samurai Castle Wing.
- Shadow recruitment.
- Masakiro defeat.
- Rising Torii Seal.
- Sakuramori Court.

Act 2: Expansion.

- More portal wings open.
- Cherry Blossom Courts become recurring safe hubs.
- The party learns seals are not simple rewards.
- Optional recruits and character quests begin.
- The villain steers the party toward a false solution.

Act 3: World Break.

- The party is tricked or forced into destabilizing the Keep.
- Safe hubs survive but become damaged.
- Routes, shops, NPCs, and enemies change.
- Masakiro returns as the Oni-Worn Lord.
- Restoration quests and deeper seal logic open.

Act 4: Restoration Or Corruption.

- The party restores or exploits major seals.
- Optional characters and endings become possible.
- Final Tower opens.
- Monster Belly and Core reveal the Keep's living truth.
- Final ending choice resolves the Core.

## Early Story Zones

Modern City Outskirts:

- Opening real-world setting.
- Establishes what normal looks like before the Keep intrudes.
- Starter-specific opening variants can frame the same route differently.

Rural Swamp Road:

- Transition from modern world into liminal danger.
- Shows reality bending before the full Keep reveal.

Swamp Outskirts:

- Tutorial route built from the current vertical slice.
- Teaches movement, combat, enemies, checkpoint, and save loop.

Castle Gate:

- First true arrival at The Black Keep.
- Damaged shrine and Witch recruitment occur here.

Samurai Castle Wing:

- First major Feudal Japan combat dungeon.
- Contains soldiers, cursed samurai, oni pressure, stealth/rescue, Shadow,
  Masakiro, and Rising Torii Seal.

Sakuramori Court:

- First Cherry Blossom Court hub.
- Peaceful when first found.
- Safe but visibly damaged after World Break.

## Hubs

All major hubs are Cherry Blossom Courts, but each is a different time-fragment
version of the idea.

First hub:

- Sakuramori Court.

Main NPC:

- High Priestess Harune.

Harune public role:

- Shrine priestess.
- Healer.
- Ritual guide.
- Hub leader.

Harune secret role:

- Hereditary vessel or guardian of the Moonpetal Seal of Time.

Hub functions:

- Character switching.
- Renaming.
- Save shrine.
- Inn or healing.
- Shop.
- Blacksmith.
- Quest board.
- Dialogue archive.
- Crafting.
- Training room.
- Bestiary.
- Palette and costume variants.
- Day/night NPC schedules.

## Time And Fast Travel

Moonpetal Seal of Time:

- Harune is secretly connected to the late-game Time seal.

Moonpetal Passage functions:

- Fast travel between discovered Cherry Blossom Courts.
- Shift hubs between day and night states.
- Access special pre-break or post-break hub quest states later.
- Stabilize final tower checkpoint floors later.

Limits:

- It should not freely rewrite the entire world.
- It should not skip locked story dungeons.
- It should only affect hubs and special quest instances.

## Traversal Seals

Traversal seals are the Black Keep progression keys.

Rules:

- One thematic seal per major battle zone.
- Safe hubs do not count as seal zones.
- Optional major zones can have optional seals.
- Optional seals unlock special areas, legendary gear, secret bosses, and secret
  characters.
- Seal rewards primarily unlock global traversal categories.
- No puzzle should require one specific character.

First seal:

- Rising Torii Seal.
- Obtained from Samurai Castle Wing.
- Unlocks vertical ascent.

## Feudal Japan Fragments

Feudal Japan is not one continuous zone. It appears as scattered time fragments
throughout The Black Keep.

Recommended fragments:

- Samurai Castle Wing: first Japan combat dungeon.
- Sakuramori Court: first Cherry Blossom Court hub.
- Bamboo Road: movement and puzzle route.
- Abandoned Shrine District: seal ritual and yokai lore.
- Oni Cave: secret boss or secret route.
- Moonlit Village: refugees and late quests.
- Red Gate Battlefield: civil war and oni invasion.
- Burning Pagoda or Storm Temple: late-game story climax.

Story layers:

- Fallen shogun.
- Oni invasion.
- Shrine curse.
- Civil war.
- Different Japan-inspired time fragments.
- Something older than The Black Keep.

## Lord Masakiro Arc

First boss:

- Lord Masakiro, Warlord of the Black Keep.

Role:

- Human warlord serving The Black Keep.
- Commands human soldiers, cursed samurai, and oni forces.
- Believes he controls the oni.
- The oni are manipulating him.

Boss phases:

1. Disciplined human warlord combat.
2. Calls soldiers and cursed samurai.
3. Oni blessing empowers him, but his control slips.
4. Defeat: oni consume him.

Later mandatory rematch:

- Masakiro, the Oni-Worn Lord.

Recommended placement:

- Revisited Samurai Castle Wing or Red Gate Battlefield.

## Stealth And Rescue

Required sequence:

1. Enter Samurai Castle Wing.
2. Learn patrol timing.
3. Pass cursed samurai watchpost.
4. Optionally find perfect-stealth treasure route.
5. Overhear oni manipulating Lord Masakiro.
6. Reach prison wing.
7. Rescue The Shadow.
8. Player names The Shadow.
9. Alarm escape sequence.
10. Three-character party combat begins.
11. Lord Masakiro boss fight.

Rule:

- Stealth can change rewards and pressure, but rescue must always remain
  possible without softlocking the story.

## World Break

The villain tricks or forces the heroes into causing the World Break.

Effects:

- The Black Keep physically changes.
- Portal worlds change.
- Safe hubs remain safe but visibly damaged.
- NPCs, shops, quests, and routes change.
- Earlier villains and zones return in corrupted forms.
- Masakiro returns as the Oni-Worn Lord.

Narrative purpose:

- Proves the seals can destabilize reality.
- Changes player understanding of the Keep.
- Gives old zones new meaning.
- Pushes the story from survival into restoration or control.

## Final Dungeon

Final dungeon order:

1. The Final Tower.
2. Inside the Monster's Belly.
3. The Core.

The Final Tower:

- Long vertical climb.
- Boss gauntlet.
- Restored-seal checkpoints.
- Heavy parallax.

Inside the Monster's Belly:

- Horror-heavy.
- The party enters the living body of the demon or Keep-heart.

The Core:

- Final boss space.
- Fusion of demon, tower, monster, and living castle heart.

Possible endings:

- Destroy.
- Seal.
- Cleanse.
- Take control.
- Bargain.
- Hidden or true option later.

## Writing Constraints

- Do not write final dialogue in planning docs.
- Use title and role names consistently.
- Keep player-given names separate from fixed titles.
- Avoid locking final character voice before recruitment and quest specs exist.
- Keep story gates tied to stable flags from technical specs.
